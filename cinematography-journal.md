## Lessons Learned
- **Exposure Triangle is real and practical**: ISO 32, shutter 1/100, f/2.4 = clean bright sunlight. Raising either ISO or slowing shutter overexposes in bright outdoor light.
- **Shutter speed affects motion smoothness**: 1/100 shutter = stuttery/laggy motion on pans. 1/50 shutter = smooth cinematic motion BUT requires darker conditions or ND filter to avoid overexposure.
- **Window light is much dimmer than direct sun**: Need to raise ISO significantly (24 → 100-400 range) to get proper exposure indoors.
- **LOG profile is essential**: Shoot in LOG (flat, washed out image) to preserve latitude for grading. Never share flat LOG footage — grade everything in DaVinci Resolve.
- **Blackmagic Camera app on iPhone 13 is professional**: Fixed f/2.4 on 13mm, f/1.6 on 26mm. ISO floor is 24. Manual control over ISO and shutter speed. Supports LOG profile.
- **Phase 00 "See" is the true beginning**: Train your eyes before touching a camera. Carry a notebook. Write 500-word Feeling Statement about emotional tone.
- **The Nolan ladder is the path**: Super 8 → 16mm → 35mm → IMAX. 31 years from age 7 to IMAX. India path: Harkat Lab, NFDC, FTII Pune, MAMI/IFFK.
